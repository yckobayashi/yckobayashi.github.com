About Me


Hello! Welcome to my personal website! My name is Yohtaro Kobayashi. My research interest lies in utilizing remote sensing technology and techniques for assessing both water quality and water quantity.
I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Geosciences at Virginia Tech. I work with Dr. George Allen in the Global Rivers Group working on estimating solar radiation, specifically the range utilized by photosynthesis, that reaches the surface of riverine waters.
Before my PhD, I had obtained my bachelors in civil engineering and a masters in environmental engineering, both at the University of Texas at El Paso in the Department of Civil Engineering. I then worked briefly as a research associate in the Texas A&M Agrilife Research Center at El Paso.
Outside of work, I enjoy cooking and baking, reading, and hiking or just enjoying nature in general!

Current Research

My current research is large scale estimation of light availability on river water surfaces. I have applied the StreamLight model to the National Hydrography Dataset for 24 hours-per-month, which can be previewed in the link below. Afterwards, I will develop a new river surface light availability model which will integrate additional factors such as complex topography.

Previous Research

A previous research project with The American Chestnut Foundation was to link the structural traits of a hybridized chestnut tree with their genetic spectrum. The American Chestnut Tree is near extinct due to a blight which was introduced in the early 1900s. While the underlying root system still exists, the blight affects the chestnut tree once it grows above ground. Ongoing attempts to bring back the American Chestnut Tree involves hybridizing the American Chestnut Tree with blight resistant chestnut trees such as the Chinese or Japanese Chestnut Trees. In order to determine how the structural traits are performing in a hybrid chestnut tree, we flew a drone with a LiDAR sensor attached to it over multiple chestnut research orchards. We then attempted to recreate digital 3D models of chestnut trees from the point cloud collected by LiDAR by processing the point cloud in the SimpleForest software.
